## Where to Find This Feature

This feature lives under the **Invoices** tab. Individual credit notes are created from the Invoice Detail page, while batch crediting is available from the Invoice List page. Credited transaction history is visible under a customer's Billing Profile in the Credit History tab.

## User Flows by Acceptance Criterion

---

### AC1 — Full or partial credit with custom text, internal comment, and visible transaction history

1. Click **Invoices** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Invoice List**.
3. You will see a table of invoices with columns for Invoice number, Customer, Date, Net, Gross, Status, and Billing Type.
4. Click any row where the status is **SENT** or **COMPLETED** to open the invoice detail page.
5. On the detail page, you will see two action buttons at the top right: **Issue Credit Note** and **Correct Invoice**.
6. Click **Issue Credit Note**.
7. You are taken to the Issue Credit Note form. At the top you choose **Full Credit** (entire invoice) or **Partial Credit** (selected rows only).
8. Optionally type a customer-facing message in the **Customer-facing message** text area. This text will appear on the credit note document.
9. Type an internal reason in the **Internal reason** text area. This field is required and will not be sent to the customer.
10. Click **Issue Credit Note**. You are redirected to the newly created credit note's detail page.
11. To view the credited transaction history, click **Customers & Config** in the top navigation bar, then **Billing Profiles** in the sidebar.
12. Click the row for the relevant customer to open their Billing Profile.
13. Click the **Credit History** tab. A table of all credit notes for this customer is shown, including the credit note number, date, original invoice reference, net and gross amounts, and status.

**Confirms requirement:** The form provides both a customer-facing text field and a required internal reason field, the credit note is issued and immediately accessible, and historical credit entries appear in the customer's Credit History tab.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC2 — Credit from a single invoice or as a separate batch run for multiple invoices

**Single invoice path:**

1. Follow steps 1–6 in AC1 above to reach the Issue Credit Note form for one invoice.
2. Complete and submit the form to create a credit note for that one invoice.

**Batch path:**

1. Click **Invoices** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Invoice List**.
3. In the invoice table, tick the checkboxes in the first column for all invoices you want to credit. Only rows with status **SENT** or **COMPLETED** can be checked.
4. A toolbar appears above the table showing how many invoices are selected, with buttons **Batch Credit**, **Set Text**, and **Clear**.
5. Click **Batch Credit**.
6. A modal dialog opens titled "Batch Credit — N invoices".
7. Optionally type a customer-facing message in the **Customer-facing message** text area.
8. Type an internal reason in the **Internal reason** text area (required).
9. Click **Issue Credits**.
10. A green banner appears above the table confirming how many credit notes succeeded and how many failed, with any failure reasons listed.

**Confirms requirement:** Both single-invoice and multi-invoice (batch) credit paths are present in the UI.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

### AC3 — FINVOICE-formatted file generated for the credit note

1. Follow AC1 steps 1–10 to create a credit note and arrive at the credit note detail page. Alternatively, navigate to any existing credit note via **Invoices → Invoice List → click a credit note row**.
2. On the invoice/credit-note detail page, scroll down to the **Attachments** section.
3. In the top-right corner of the Attachments section, you will see a button labelled **↓ FINVOICE XML**.
4. Click **↓ FINVOICE XML**. The browser downloads an XML file in FINVOICE format for this credit note.
5. If the file is not yet available, a browser alert will say "FINVOICE XML not available for this invoice."

**Confirms requirement:** The FINVOICE XML download button is present on every invoice/credit-note detail page and retrieves the FINVOICE-formatted file for that document.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC4 — Credit note is assigned the number of the invoice being credited

1. Follow AC1 steps 1–10. After submission, you land on the credit note detail page.
2. The page header shows the invoice number (e.g. "Invoice 2024-001"). If the credit note inherits the original invoice number, it will appear here. The orange banner at the top says **CREDIT NOTE for Invoice #[original ID]**.
3. On the original invoice's detail page, scroll to the top. If a credit note has been issued, a yellow banner says "This invoice has been (partially) credited" and lists the credit note number as a clickable link (e.g. "2024-001-C" or the credit note's assigned number).

**Confirms requirement:** The credit note number is displayed on both the credit note detail page and the original invoice's detail page. However, the UI does not explicitly state that the credit note uses the same series number as the original — the number shown depends entirely on what the backend assigns, and the frontend does not label this rule anywhere.

**UI Status:** ⚠️ PARTIAL

**What is missing:** The UI shows the credit note's invoice number but does not communicate to the user that the number derives from the credited invoice's number. A user relying only on the UI cannot verify this rule without checking the number manually.

### AC5 — Credit note for entire invoice or only for selected invoice rows

1. Click **Invoices** in the top navigation bar, then **Invoice List** in the sidebar.
2. Click a SENT or COMPLETED invoice row.
3. Click **Issue Credit Note** at the top of the detail page.
4. On the Issue Credit Note form, you will see two radio buttons: **Full Credit** and **Partial Credit**.
5. Select **Full Credit** to credit the entire invoice with no row selection needed.
6. Select **Partial Credit** to reveal a table labelled "Select Line Items to Credit", showing every line item with columns Description, Qty, Net, and Gross. Each row has a checkbox.
7. Tick the checkboxes for the specific line items you wish to credit.
8. Complete the text fields and click **Issue Credit Note**.

**Confirms requirement:** Both full-invoice and row-level partial credit options are clearly presented as radio buttons, with a line-item selection table appearing for the partial option.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

### AC7 — Text can be added to the credit note

1. Navigate to an invoice detail page (Invoices → Invoice List → click a SENT/COMPLETED row).
2. Click **Issue Credit Note**.
3. On the Issue Credit Note form, locate the field labelled **Customer-facing message (optional)**.
4. Type any text into this field. A placeholder reads "Visible on the credit note…".
5. Submit the form. The entered text is sent as the `customText` field in the credit note request.

**Confirms requirement:** A clearly labelled optional text field is present on the Issue Credit Note form for adding a customer-visible message to the credit note.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC8 — An internal note can be written in connection with the credit note

1. Navigate to an invoice detail page (Invoices → Invoice List → click a SENT/COMPLETED row).
2. Click **Issue Credit Note**.
3. On the Issue Credit Note form, locate the field labelled **Internal reason** (marked with a red asterisk — it is required).
4. Beneath the label, a note reads: "This will NOT be sent to the customer. It is for internal records only."
5. Type your internal reason into the text area.
6. Submit the form. The form will reject submission if this field is left blank, showing the error "Internal comment is required".

**Confirms requirement:** An internal-only comment field is mandatory on the Issue Credit Note form and is clearly labeled as not visible to the customer.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

### AC9 — Historical entries of transactions stored in transaction records

1. Click **Customers & Config** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Billing Profiles**.
3. You see a list of customers. Click the row for the customer whose credit history you want to review.
4. On the Billing Profile page, click the **Credit History** tab (between the "Profile" and "Change History" tabs).
5. A table appears with columns: Credit Note #, Date, Original Invoice #, Net, Gross, Status. All credit notes issued for this customer are listed here with amounts shown in red.
6. Click any credit note row to navigate directly to that credit note's detail page.

**Confirms requirement:** All credit transactions for a customer are stored and visible in the Credit History tab of that customer's Billing Profile page.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

### AC10 — Use case 1: customer already paid — separate credit note issued and refund given

1. Click **Invoices** in the top navigation bar, then **Invoice List** in the sidebar.
2. Find an invoice with status **SENT** or **COMPLETED** (indicating the invoice has been sent and likely paid). Click the row.
3. On the Invoice Detail page, click **Issue Credit Note** in the top-right button group.
4. On the Issue Credit Note form, select **Full Credit** (or Partial Credit depending on the overcharged amount).
5. Enter a customer-facing message describing the refund reason (optional) and a required internal reason.
6. Click **Issue Credit Note**. You are taken to the new credit note detail page.
7. The credit note shows a banner "CREDIT NOTE for Invoice #[original]" at the top.

**Confirms requirement:** The flow directly supports creating a separate credit note for any sent invoice, covering the scenario where a customer has already paid an incorrect invoice.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC11 — Use case 2: partial credit before customer has paid

1. Click **Invoices** in the top navigation bar, then **Invoice List** in the sidebar.
2. Find an invoice with status **SENT** (not yet paid). Click the row.
3. On the Invoice Detail page, click **Issue Credit Note**.
4. On the Issue Credit Note form, select **Partial Credit**.
5. A table of line items appears. Tick only the checkboxes for the rows you wish to credit.
6. Enter an optional customer-facing message and a required internal reason.
7. Click **Issue Credit Note**.
8. You are taken to the credit note detail page. The original invoice's detail page will show a yellow "partially credited" banner linking to the credit note.

**Confirms requirement:** The Partial Credit option with row-level selection directly supports crediting only part of the invoice transactions before the customer has paid.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

## Backend-Only Aspects

AC4 involves the assignment of the credit note number. The business rule (credit note receives the number of the invoice being credited) is enforced entirely in the backend at the time the credit note is created. The frontend simply displays whatever invoice number is returned — it does not apply or communicate this numbering rule to the user.
