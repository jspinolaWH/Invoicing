## Where to Find This Feature

This feature spans three areas of the app. The flag that marks a product as subject to reverse charge VAT is set in **Master Data → Products**. The effect on individual billing events is visible in the **Operations → Billing Events** detail page. The final invoice view and FINVOICE export are accessed under **Invoices → Invoice List**.

## User Flows by Acceptance Criterion

---

### AC1 — Mark a product as subject to reverse charge VAT

1. Click **Master Data** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Products**.
3. You will see a table of all products. Each row shows the product code, pricing unit, and a **Reverse Charge VAT** column. Products flagged with reverse charge display a purple **Reverse Charge** badge; all others show a dash.
4. To mark a new product as reverse charge: click **+ Add Product** in the top-right corner. A modal opens.
5. In the modal, fill in the **Product Code** and **Pricing Unit** fields, then check the **Reverse Charge VAT** checkbox (labelled "B2B — VAT liability shifts to buyer").
6. Click **Save**. The modal closes and the product appears in the table with a **Reverse Charge** badge in the Reverse Charge VAT column.
7. To update an existing product: click **Edit** on any row, toggle the **Reverse Charge VAT** checkbox, and click **Save**.

**Confirms requirement:** The Products page allows any product to be designated as subject to reverse charge VAT, enabling the system to know which goods (e.g. scrap metal, construction waste) should trigger reverse charge invoicing.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC2 — Automatic reverse charge application shown on a billing event

1. Click **Operations** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Billing Events**.
3. You will see a list of billing events. Click the **View** button on any event whose product has the Reverse Charge VAT flag set.
4. On the event detail page, click the **Details** tab (it is active by default).
5. Scroll down to the **VAT Details** section (shown when a VAT rate or calculated amount is present). The section displays:
   - **VAT Rate Code** — the resolved VAT code for this event date
   - **Effective Rate** — the percentage applied (0% for reverse charge)
   - **Reverse Charge** — shows **Yes** (highlighted in amber) when the event's product carries the reverse charge flag
   - **Buyer VAT #** — shown if a buyer VAT number is stored on the event
   - **Net Amount**, **VAT Amount**, **Gross Amount** — the calculated figures

**Confirms requirement:** The system automatically resolves the reverse charge flag from the product and shows it on the event, demonstrating that VAT liability has been transferred to the buyer without any manual step by the user.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC3 — A single invoice can include both standard and reverse charge lines

1. Click **Invoices** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Invoice List**.
3. You will see a list of invoices. Click on any invoice row to open the invoice detail page.
4. On the invoice detail page, scroll to the **Line Items** table. Each row shows:
   - Description, Quantity, Unit Price, **VAT %**, Net Amount, Gross Amount, Classification, Account, Cost Center
5. If an invoice contains a mix of billing events — some with standard VAT (e.g. 24%) and some with 0% VAT for reverse charge — each line item shows its own VAT rate in the **VAT %** column. Different rates appear side by side within the same invoice.
6. Scroll further to the **amount summary bar** beneath the table. If any line carries reverse charge VAT, a purple **Reverse Charge** badge is shown alongside the Net, VAT, and Gross totals.

**Confirms requirement:** The line items table displays each event's VAT rate independently, showing that one invoice can simultaneously carry lines at different VAT rates, including a 0% reverse charge line and a standard 24% line.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC4 — Invoice shows buyer's VAT number and clear reverse charge indication

1. Follow steps 1–3 from AC3 above to open an invoice detail page.
2. Scroll to the **amount summary bar** directly below the line items table. When the invoice contains reverse charge VAT lines, a purple **Reverse Charge** badge is displayed prominently in the summary row.
3. The buyer's VAT number is visible in the billing event that feeds the invoice. To see it: click **Operations → Billing Events**, find the relevant event, click **View**, and scroll to the **VAT Details** section — the **Buyer VAT #** field shows the buyer's VAT registration number.
4. The FINVOICE XML (downloaded from the **Attachments** section on the invoice detail page — click **↓ FINVOICE XML**) carries the mandatory reverse charge tax code and buyer VAT number in the export payload.

**Confirms requirement:** The reverse charge badge on the invoice amount summary provides a clear on-screen indication. The buyer VAT number is recorded and visible on the source billing event, and both are included in the FINVOICE export.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC5 — Reverse charge VAT transactions visible in VAT reporting

1. Click **Master Data** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Overview**.
3. You will see the Master Data hub with cards for each area. Find and click the **VAT Report** card (described as "Standard VAT totals and reverse charge VAT base totals by date range").
4. The VAT Report page opens. Set a **From** date and a **To** date using the date pickers, then click **Generate Report**.
5. The report shows three summary tiles:
   - **Standard VAT Total** — total VAT collected on standard invoices in the period
   - **Reverse Charge Base Total** — net base amount subject to reverse charge VAT (the taxable base transferred to the buyer)
   - **Reverse Charge Invoices** — count of invoices with reverse charge VAT applied in the period

**Confirms requirement:** The VAT Report page explicitly separates reverse charge VAT totals from standard VAT, allowing the waste company to report these transactions as required by tax authorities.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC6 — Reverse charge VAT exported in FINVOICE format

1. Click **Invoices** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Invoice List**.
3. Click on any invoice to open the invoice detail page.
4. Scroll down to the **Attachments** section.
5. Click the **↓ FINVOICE XML** button in the top-right of the Attachments panel.
6. The browser downloads an XML file named `invoice-[number].xml`. This file is the FINVOICE-format export. For reverse charge invoices, the backend includes the mandatory reverse charge tax code and buyer VAT number in the XML metadata.

**Confirms requirement:** The FINVOICE XML download button is present on every invoice detail page. The backend generates the XML including reverse charge VAT metadata as part of the invoice data transfer to external invoicing systems.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC7 — All reverse charge invoices are recorded (auditability)

1. Click **Invoices** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Invoice List**.
3. Click on any invoice to open the invoice detail page.
4. Scroll to the bottom of the page to the **Reporting Data Audit Trail** section.
5. The table lists every reporting-field entry for this invoice, with columns for **Line Item**, **Field**, **Assigned Value**, **Logged At**, and **Logged By**. Reverse charge flag values are recorded as reporting fields and appear in this audit log.
6. Additionally, on the source billing event: click **Operations → Billing Events**, open the event, and click the **Audit Trail** tab. Every change to the event — including reverse charge flag resolution — is timestamped and attributed to a user or system.

**Confirms requirement:** Both the invoice-level reporting data audit trail and the billing event audit trail together provide a full record of all invoices and events where reverse charge VAT was applied, satisfying the auditability requirement.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

## Backend-Only Aspects

None — all acceptance criteria have a visible UI surface.

The automatic application of reverse charge VAT (AC2) is driven by backend logic that reads the product's `reverseChargeVat` flag at event creation time, but the result is clearly visible in the Billing Event Detail page's VAT Details section. The FINVOICE export (AC6) is generated by the backend, but it is triggered by the user via the download button on the invoice detail page.
