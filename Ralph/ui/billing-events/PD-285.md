## Where to Find This Feature

This feature spans two areas of the app. Configuration lives under **Customers & Config** → **Classification Rules**, where you define which customer types, products, or regions map to Public Law or Private Law. The results appear under **Operations** → **Billing Events** (event list and detail) and **Invoices** → **Invoice List** (line items on an invoice detail page), and the FINVOICE export is accessible from any invoice detail page.

## User Flows by Acceptance Criterion

---

### AC1 — System supports both private-law and public-law invoicing

1. Click **Customers & Config** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Classification Rules**.
3. You will see a table of rules with columns for Priority, Label, Customer Type, Product Code, Region, Result (PUBLIC_LAW or PRIVATE_LAW), Active, and Actions.
4. The presence of rules producing both PUBLIC_LAW and PRIVATE_LAW results confirms that the system is configured to handle both legal frameworks.
5. To verify classification is applied to events, click **Operations** in the top navigation bar, then click **Billing Events** in the sidebar.
6. Click the **View** button on any billing event row.
7. On the Details tab you will see a field labelled **Classification** showing either "PUBLIC_LAW", "PRIVATE_LAW", or a dash if not yet classified.

**Confirms requirement:** The Classification Rules page shows two possible result values (PUBLIC_LAW and PRIVATE_LAW), and each billing event detail shows the applied classification.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC2 — Two primary ways to handle public-law and private-law invoicing

1. **Automatic (rule-based):** Click **Customers & Config** in the top navigation bar, then click **Classification Rules** in the sidebar. Rules are evaluated against customer type, product code, and region — the matching rule assigns the classification automatically when an event is processed.
2. **Event-level field (visible result):** Click **Operations**, then **Billing Events**, then **View** on any event. On the **Details** tab, the **Classification** field shows the classification that was applied to that specific event.

**Confirms requirement:** Two entry points exist — the rules engine (automatic determination) and the per-event Classification field (visible outcome for each transaction).

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC3 — System automatically distinguishes between public-law and private-law transactions

1. Click **Customers & Config** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Classification Rules**.
3. You will see the rule table. Each rule has conditions: **Customer Type** (e.g. MUNICIPALITY for public-law services), **Product Code** (specific waste collection product), and **Region**.
4. Click **+ Add Rule** to open the rule creation modal.
5. You will see dropdown fields for Customer Type Condition (options include MUNICIPALITY, AUTHORITY, PRIVATE, BUSINESS), Product Code Condition, and Region Condition.
6. Set **Result Classification** to PUBLIC_LAW or PRIVATE_LAW, assign a Priority, and click **Save**.
7. The system will automatically apply this rule to new billing events that match the conditions.

**Confirms requirement:** Classification rules with customer-type, product, and region conditions enable the system to automatically assign the correct legal classification without manual intervention for each event.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC4 — Invoice creation applies statutory classification rules to determine separate or combined invoicing

1. Click **Customers & Config** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Classification Rules**.
3. Rules are evaluated in priority order — the highest-priority matching rule determines the classification.
4. To see the resulting classification on an event after invoicing, click **Operations**, then **Billing Events**, then **View** on any event.
5. On the **Details** tab, the **Classification** field shows the rule-resolved value (PUBLIC_LAW or PRIVATE_LAW).
6. On an invoice, click **Invoices** in the top navigation bar, then **Invoice List** in the sidebar.
7. Click any invoice row to open the invoice detail page.
8. Scroll down to the **Line Items** table. Each row has a **Classification** column showing a colour-coded badge: "Public Law" (blue) or "Private Law" (orange) per line item.

**Confirms requirement:** The classification determined by the rules engine at event time flows through to each individual invoice line item, confirming statutory rules are applied at invoice creation.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC5 — Correct legal collection processes, VAT, and enforcement mechanisms applied

1. Click **Operations** in the top navigation bar, then click **Billing Events** in the sidebar.
2. Click **View** on any event to open its detail page.
3. On the **Details** tab, review the **Classification** field (PUBLIC_LAW or PRIVATE_LAW) alongside the **VAT Details** section, which shows the resolved VAT rate, effective rate percentage, and reverse charge flag.
4. The combination of classification and VAT details shows that the correct VAT treatment is applied depending on the legal framework.

**Confirms requirement:** The billing event detail shows both the legal classification and the resolved VAT rate in a single view, indicating that VAT application follows the classification. Enforcement routing is performed automatically by the backend based on the classification; there is no user-visible enforcement screen.

**UI Status:** ⚠️ PARTIAL

**What is missing from the UI:** Enforcement mechanism routing (i.e., which collection authority handles this invoice) is not visible in the UI. The system applies it automatically in the backend based on the classification, but the user cannot see or configure which enforcement path was chosen for a specific invoice.

### AC6 — System supports a single invoice including both public-law and private-law transactions

1. Click **Invoices** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Invoice List**.
3. Click any invoice row to open the invoice detail page.
4. Scroll down to the **Line Items** table.
5. You will see multiple rows — each row has a **Classification** column. Different rows on the same invoice can show "Public Law" (blue badge) and "Private Law" (orange badge), confirming that a single invoice can contain both types of transactions.

**Confirms requirement:** The Line Items table on an invoice can simultaneously display rows classified as Public Law and rows classified as Private Law, proving that mixed invoices are supported.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC7 — Each invoice line includes a legally compliant classification identifier

1. Click **Invoices** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Invoice List**.
3. Click any invoice row to open the invoice detail page.
4. Scroll down to the **Line Items** table.
5. You will see a column labelled **Classification** on every row. Each cell displays one of:
   - A blue **Public Law** badge (for PUBLIC_LAW transactions)
   - An orange **Private Law** badge (for PRIVATE_LAW transactions)
   - A grey **Unclassified** badge (if no classification has been resolved yet)

**Confirms requirement:** Every invoice line item has a visible classification badge, providing a per-line legal identifier.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC8 — FINVOICE export attaches metadata indicating public/private law per transaction

1. Click **Invoices** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Invoice List**.
3. Click any invoice row to open the invoice detail page.
4. Scroll down to the **Attachments** section.
5. You will see a button labelled **↓ FINVOICE XML** in the top-right corner of the Attachments panel.
6. Click **↓ FINVOICE XML**. The browser will download an XML file named after the invoice number.
7. Open the downloaded XML file. Each invoice line item within the FINVOICE XML includes the legal classification metadata (PUBLIC_LAW or PRIVATE_LAW), indicating how each transaction is categorised for external processing and collection.

**Confirms requirement:** The FINVOICE XML download is directly accessible from the invoice detail page, and the generated XML includes per-line classification metadata for external invoicing and collection systems.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

## Backend-Only Aspects

**Enforcement mechanism routing (AC5 partial):** When an invoice is created with a PUBLIC_LAW classification, the backend automatically routes it to the appropriate statutory collection authority. This is handled entirely in the backend based on the classification value; there is no user-facing screen showing which enforcement path was chosen or allowing it to be configured per invoice. The classification itself is visible, but the downstream enforcement routing is opaque to the user in the current UI.
