## Where to Find This Feature

This feature is spread across several areas of the app. Configuration lives under **Master Data** (VAT Rates, Accounting Accounts) and **Customers & Config** (Classification Rules, Validation Rules, Billing Profiles). The results of that configuration are visible on each Invoice detail page, which shows line-item reporting data and an audit trail, and provides the button to transmit the invoice to the external operator system.

## User Flows by Acceptance Criterion

---

### AC1 — System automatically integrates reporting data into the invoice file

1. Click **Invoices** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Invoice List**.
3. You will see a table of all invoices. Click on any invoice row to open its detail page.
4. On the detail page, scroll to the **Line Items** section.
5. You will see a table where each row represents one billing event. The columns include Description, Quantity, Unit Price, VAT %, Net amount, Gross amount, Classification, Account, and Cost Center — all populated automatically from the customer and event records.

**Confirms requirement:** Each invoice line shows pre-populated accounting, VAT, and classification data without any manual entry by the user.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC2 — Mandatory reporting info automatically assigned to invoice lines

1. Click **Invoices** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Invoice List**.
3. Click on any invoice to open its detail page.
4. In the **Line Items** table you will see columns: **Classification** (showing PUBLIC LAW or PRIVATE LAW badges), **Account** (ledger code), **Cost Center**, and **VAT %** — all filled automatically per line.
5. Below the line items, the **Amount Summary** bar shows Net Amount, VAT Amount, and Gross Amount totals.

**Confirms requirement:** The Classification, Account, and VAT fields on every line item confirm that mandatory reporting data has been assigned automatically.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC3 — Integration between WasteHero and the invoicing system (FINVOICE export)

1. Click **Invoices** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Invoice List**.
3. Click on an invoice that has status **READY** or **SENT**.
4. Scroll to the **Transmission & Integration** section near the bottom of the page.
5. For a READY invoice, click **Transmit to Operator**. The system sends the invoice (in FINVOICE format) to the external operator system and shows a confirmation with an external reference number and timestamp.
6. For a SENT invoice, you can click **Check Delivery Status**, **View Invoice Image (PDF)**, **Fetch External Attachments**, or **Retransmit (Updated Address)**.

**Confirms requirement:** The "Transmit to Operator" button and subsequent delivery status/image retrieval confirm that the invoice file is exported to the external invoicing system.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC4 — Reporting data predefined at customer or service level

**Customer level:**

1. Click **Customers & Config** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Billing Profiles**.
3. Click any customer row to open their billing profile.
4. Scroll to the **Reporting Defaults** section.
5. You will see a **Default Legal Classification** dropdown (Public Law / Private Law) and a **Default Ledger Code** text field. These values are applied automatically to all invoices for this customer without manual input each time.
6. Click **Save** to persist the settings.

**Service/product level:**

1. Click **Master Data** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Products**.
3. Click **Edit** on any product row.
4. In the modal, you will see a **Default Account** field (linked to an accounting account) and a **Default Cost Center** field. These defaults are applied automatically to any billing event using this product.
5. Click **Save** to persist.

**Confirms requirement:** Both the Billing Profile page and the Products page expose fields for configuring reporting data at the customer and service levels.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

### AC5 — Transaction type classification: public law or private law

1. Click **Customers & Config** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Classification Rules**.
3. You will see a table of rules. Each rule has: Priority, Customer Type condition, Product Code condition, Region condition, and a **Result** column showing either PUBLIC LAW or PRIVATE LAW.
4. Click **+ Add Rule** to open a form where you define the conditions and select the resulting classification.
5. Alternatively, click **Edit** on an existing rule to change the conditions.
6. The system evaluates these rules in priority order for each billing event and marks the resulting invoice line with the correct classification badge.

**Confirms requirement:** The Classification Rules page lets users define which events are public law or private law; the result is visible per line on the Invoice Detail page.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC6 — Ledger account mapping per service

1. Click **Master Data** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Accounting**.
3. You will see a table of accounting accounts, each with a Code, Name, Valid From date, and optional Valid To date.
4. Click **+ Add Account** to create a new ledger account (Code and Name are required).
5. To link an account to a product/service: click **Products** in the left sidebar under Master Data.
6. Click **Edit** on a product. In the **Default Account** field, type to search and select the desired accounting account.
7. This account code then appears automatically in the **Account** column of every invoice line that uses this product.

**Confirms requirement:** Accounting Accounts master data and the Default Account field on products confirm that each service is linked to a predefined ledger account.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC7 — VAT amounts itemised per invoice line

1. Click **Invoices** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Invoice List**.
3. Click any invoice row to open its detail.
4. In the **Line Items** table, note the **VAT %** column — each line shows its applicable VAT rate.
5. The **Net** and **Gross** columns reflect the net amount and gross amount (net + VAT) per line.
6. Below the table, the **Amount Summary** bar shows the total Net Amount, VAT Amount, and Gross Amount for the entire invoice.

To configure VAT rates themselves:
1. Click **Master Data** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **VAT Rates**.
3. You will see all defined VAT rates with their codes and percentages. Click **+ Add VAT Rate** to add a new rate.

**Confirms requirement:** The VAT % column on each line item, combined with the amount summary, confirms that VAT is itemised per invoice line.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC8 — Error prevention: required reporting data verified before finalising

1. Click **Customers & Config** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Validation Rules**.
3. You will see a table of validation rules. Look for rules with type **REPORTING_DATA_COMPLETENESS** — these rules instruct the system to check that all required reporting fields are present before an invoice is finalised.
4. Also look for **VAT_ACCURACY** rules, which verify that VAT breakdowns are correct.
5. Click **+ Add Rule** to create a new validation rule. Select the rule type from the dropdown (including REPORTING_DATA_COMPLETENESS and VAT_ACCURACY), enter a rule code, choose whether it is blocking (prevents invoice generation) or non-blocking, and click **Save**.
6. During invoice generation or billing runs, any invoice that fails a blocking validation rule is held back and the failure is reported in the validation summary.

**Confirms requirement:** The REPORTING_DATA_COMPLETENESS and VAT_ACCURACY validation rule types confirm that the system verifies required reporting data before finalising invoices.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC9 — Audit and compliance: all reporting data logged

1. Click **Invoices** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Invoice List**.
3. Click any invoice to open its detail page.
4. Scroll to the bottom of the page to the **Reporting Data Audit Trail** section.
5. You will see a table with columns: Line Item, Field, Assigned Value, Logged At, and Logged By.
6. Every reporting field value assigned to every line item is recorded here with a timestamp and the user or process that assigned it.

**Confirms requirement:** The Reporting Data Audit Trail table at the bottom of the Invoice Detail page provides a complete, timestamped record of all assigned reporting data.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE
