## Where to Find This Feature

This feature lives under the **Operations** tab. The Seasonal Fees configuration is accessed through the **Seasonal Fees** sidebar item, and the billing events that are automatically generated from those configurations appear in the **Billing Events** list. The audit trail for each generated event is on the event's detail page.

## User Flows by Acceptance Criterion

---

### AC1 — Recurring fees auto-recognised as billable events

1. Click **Operations** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Seasonal Fees**.
3. You will see a table listing all configured seasonal fee rules, with columns for Customer Number, Product, Frequency, Amount, Next Due Date, Property Reference, and Active status.
4. Each active row has a **Generate Now** button. Clicking it immediately creates a new billing event for that rule and shows a confirmation message (e.g. "Event #42 created. Next due: 2027-01-01").
5. The Next Due Date column updates automatically to the next period after generation.
6. Click **Operations** in the top navigation bar and then click **Billing Events** in the sidebar to confirm the newly generated event appears in the list with its status and origin badge.

**Confirms requirement:** Configured seasonal fee rules automatically produce billing events — no manual data entry is needed.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC2 — Automatic identification of time-based fees

1. Click **Operations** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Seasonal Fees**.
3. The page lists all non-event-based fee configurations (annual property charges, quarterly rents, monthly fees) along with their Next Due Date.
4. For any active rule, click **Generate Now**. The system immediately recognises the scheduled fee and creates a billing event. A confirmation message appears below the row confirming the event ID and the new next due date.
5. Billing events created this way appear in the **Billing Events** list with an origin badge that identifies them as system-generated.

**Confirms requirement:** The system recognises and processes time-based billing items and creates billing events for them on demand (and automatically on the scheduled date).

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC3 — Configurable billing rules: frequency selection

1. Click **Operations** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Seasonal Fees**.
3. Click **+ Add Seasonal Fee** in the top right corner of the page.
4. A modal dialog opens with the following required fields: Customer Number, Product (dropdown from master data), Billing Frequency (dropdown with options MONTHLY, QUARTERLY, ANNUAL), Amount (in euros, net), Next Due Date, and optional Property Reference and Description.
5. Select the desired **Billing Frequency** from the dropdown.
6. Fill in the remaining required fields and click **Save**.
7. The new rule appears in the table. A green success message "Seasonal fee created." confirms the save.

**Confirms requirement:** Users can define billing frequencies (monthly, quarterly, annually) and link them to a product, satisfying the configurable billing rules requirement.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC4 — Flexible fee management: update fees dynamically

1. Click **Operations** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Seasonal Fees**.
3. Find the seasonal fee rule you wish to update and click **Edit** in its row.
4. The Edit Seasonal Fee modal opens pre-filled with the current values.
5. Change the **Amount** field (e.g. updating an annual base fee for the new year) and adjust the **Next Due Date** if needed.
6. Click **Save**. A green success message "Seasonal fee updated." confirms the change.
7. The updated amount and date are immediately visible in the table row.

**Confirms requirement:** Seasonal fees can be revised annually or at any time without creating new records for each billing period — the single rule is updated and all future events are generated from the new values.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC5 — Customer-specific fee configuration per property type

1. Click **Operations** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Seasonal Fees**.
3. Click **+ Add Seasonal Fee** to open the creation modal.
4. Enter a specific **Customer Number** (e.g. for a permanent residence customer) and fill in the fee amount and frequency for that property type (e.g. ANNUAL, €150, invoiced January).
5. Click **Save** to create a rule for that customer.
6. Repeat the process for a different customer (e.g. a holiday home) with a different amount and month in the Next Due Date field.
7. Use the **Filter by customer** text box at the top of the page to confirm that each customer has its own separate rule with distinct amounts and frequencies.
8. The optional **Property Reference** field allows tying a rule to a specific property on a customer's account.

**Confirms requirement:** Different customer types (permanent residence, holiday home, shared collection) can each have separate billing rules with their own amounts and schedules.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC6 — Seamless integration with invoicing workflows

1. Click **Operations** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Billing Events**.
3. You will see a table of all billing events. Events generated from seasonal fee rules appear in the same list as event-based billing events.
4. Check the **Origin** column — events generated by the seasonal fee scheduler show a system-generated origin badge.
5. These events go through the same status lifecycle (DRAFT → IN_PROGRESS → SENT → COMPLETED) and can be validated, excluded, or transferred just like any other billing event.
6. Click **View** on any seasonal-fee-generated event to open the detail page and confirm its full details, status, and processing history.

**Confirms requirement:** Seasonal fee billing events are processed identically to other billing events, ensuring consistency in financial reporting and invoicing.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC7 — Auditing and compliance: audit trail for generated charges

1. Click **Operations** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Billing Events**.
3. Find a billing event that was generated by a seasonal fee rule and click **View** to open the event detail page.
4. Click the **Audit Trail** tab at the top of the detail page.
5. You will see a filterable table with columns: Timestamp, User, Field, Previous Value, New Value, and Reason.
6. All changes to the event — including its initial creation, any edits, status transitions, or exclusions — are recorded here with the user who made them and when.
7. Use the filter controls (by user, field, date range) to narrow the audit trail for compliance review.

**Confirms requirement:** Every automatically generated charge and subsequent change has a clear audit trail showing who did what and when, satisfying regulatory compliance and internal audit requirements.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC8 — Worked example: permanent/holiday/shared collection billing rules

1. Click **Operations** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Seasonal Fees**.
3. Create three seasonal fee rules using the **+ Add Seasonal Fee** button:
   - Permanent residence customer: ANNUAL frequency, amount €150.00, Next Due Date set to January of the billing year.
   - Holiday home customer: ANNUAL frequency, amount €75.00, Next Due Date set to April of the billing year.
   - Shared collection customer: MONTHLY frequency, amount €10.00, Next Due Date set to the first day of the current month.
4. All three rules appear in the table, each with its own customer number, frequency, amount, and next due date.
5. Clicking **Generate Now** on any rule creates the corresponding billing event immediately. The system shows the new event ID and the updated next due date.
6. Navigate to **Billing Events** to confirm all three events appear and are processing normally.

**Confirms requirement:** The exact use case from the requirement (permanent residences, holiday homes, shared collection with different frequencies and amounts) is fully configurable and produces billing events automatically.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE
