## Where to Find This Feature

This feature lives primarily under the **Operations** tab. The core configuration is accessed via **Restrictions** in the sidebar, with supplementary per-customer scheduling available in **Billing Cycles**. Customer-level invoice grouping is accessed from a customer's billing profile page.

## User Flows by Acceptance Criterion

---

### AC1 — Define invoicing rules at service and customer levels

1. Click **Operations** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Restrictions**.
3. You will see a table of existing billing restriction rules. Each row shows a Billing Type, Service Type, Municipality, Customer Type, Minimum Amount, Period, and whether the rule is Active.
4. Click **+ Add Restriction** in the top-right corner of the page.
5. A modal dialog opens titled "Add Billing Restriction".
6. The modal contains fields for: Billing Type, Service Type, Municipality, Customer Type, Minimum Amount, Period, Service Responsibility, and Description. These fields let you target a rule at a specific service category, customer class, or location.
7. Fill in the desired values and click **Save**.
8. You will see a green confirmation message "Restriction created." and the new rule appears in the table.

**Confirms requirement:** Users can create rules scoped to specific service types and customer types, satisfying both the service-level and customer-level targeting described in the requirement.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

### AC2 — Billing interval: immediate invoicing vs scheduled (cycle-based)

1. Click **Operations** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Restrictions**.
3. Click **+ Add Restriction**.
4. In the modal, locate the **Billing Type** dropdown (the first field, marked with an asterisk as required).
5. The dropdown offers two options: **CYCLE_BASED** (for scheduled recurring invoicing) and **IMMEDIATE** (for on-demand invoicing triggered immediately after the event).
6. Select **IMMEDIATE** for services such as septic tank emptying, or **CYCLE_BASED** for recurring waste collection services.
7. Fill in the remaining fields as needed (such as Service Type to scope the rule) and click **Save**.
8. In the restrictions table, the Billing Type column will show the selected value. Rows with IMMEDIATE are highlighted in amber to visually distinguish them from cycle-based rules.

**Confirms requirement:** The two billing interval modes — immediate and scheduled — are directly selectable via the Billing Type field on the Billing Restrictions page.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

### AC3 — Service types with separate invoicing criteria

1. Click **Operations** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Restrictions**.
3. Click **+ Add Restriction**.
4. In the modal, find the **Service Type** field.
5. Enter a service type identifier (for example, type `SEPTIC_TANK_EMPTYING` for septic services or `CONTAINER_EMPTYING` for mixed waste collection).
6. Set the appropriate **Billing Type** for this service (for example, IMMEDIATE for septic tank services).
7. Click **Save**.
8. Repeat to create a second restriction for a different service type with a different billing type.
9. Both rules will appear in the table, each with a distinct Service Type and Billing Type, confirming that different service categories can have different invoicing criteria.

**Confirms requirement:** The Service Type field allows separate restriction rules to be defined per service category, directly matching the requirement for service-type-specific invoicing criteria.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

### AC4 — Customer preferences: customised invoicing options

1. Click **Operations** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Restrictions**.
3. Click **+ Add Restriction**.
4. In the modal, use the **Customer Type** dropdown to scope the rule to RESIDENTIAL, BUSINESS, or MUNICIPAL customers (or leave as "— any —" for all customers).
5. Combine this with a specific Service Type and Billing Type to create a customer-contract-aware invoicing rule.
6. Click **Save**.

Additionally, for per-customer scheduling:
7. In the left sidebar, click **Billing Cycles**.
8. Click **+ Add Cycle**.
9. In the modal, enter the **Customer Number**, choose a **Frequency** (MONTHLY, QUARTERLY, SEMI_ANNUAL, or ANNUAL), set the **Next Billing Date**, and optionally a **Service Type** to scope the cycle.
10. Click **Save**. The cycle now governs when invoices are generated for that specific customer and service combination.

**Confirms requirement:** Customer Type scoping in Billing Restrictions and per-customer billing cycles in Billing Cycles together satisfy the customised invoicing options per customer contract.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

### AC5 — Invoice grouping; prevent conflicting groupings

For invoice grouping (bundling):
1. Click **Customers & Config** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Billing Profiles**.
3. Click any customer row to open their billing profile.
4. Near the top of the page, click the **Bundling Rules** button.
5. You will see a table showing Product Groups and their Bundling Type (either SINGLE_LINE to consolidate events into one invoice line, or SEPARATE to list them individually).
6. To add a rule, enter a product group name (such as `CONTAINER_EMPTYING`) in the "Add Product Group" field, choose SINGLE_LINE or SEPARATE, then click **Add Row**.
7. When done, click **Save Changes**.

For conflict prevention (preventing immediate-invoiced events from being delayed by cycle-based events):
This logic is applied automatically by the backend when invoice runs are processed. There is no separate UI configuration for conflict prevention — the IMMEDIATE billing type restriction already signals to the system that those events must be invoiced without waiting for a scheduled cycle.

**Confirms requirement:** Invoice grouping rules are fully visible and configurable through the Bundling Rules page. The conflict-prevention aspect (no delay for IMMEDIATE events) is handled automatically with no additional UI needed.

**UI Status:** ⚠️ PARTIAL

The grouping UI is complete. However, there is no explicit UI screen showing "conflict prevention" logic or confirming that IMMEDIATE events are shielded from cycle delays. That enforcement is backend-only.

### AC8 — Example scenario: semi-annual for mixed waste, immediate for septic tank

This scenario is exercised by combining two pages:

Step 1 — Set up immediate invoicing for septic tank emptying:
1. Click **Operations** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Restrictions**.
3. Click **+ Add Restriction**.
4. Set Billing Type to **IMMEDIATE**, Service Type to `SEPTIC_TANK_EMPTYING`.
5. Click **Save**.

Step 2 — Set up semi-annual billing for mixed waste collection:
6. In the left sidebar, click **Billing Cycles**.
7. Click **+ Add Cycle**.
8. Enter the customer's number, set Frequency to **SEMI_ANNUAL**, set Service Type to `MIXED_WASTE`, enter the next billing date.
9. Click **Save**.

The system will now treat septic tank events as requiring immediate invoicing and group mixed waste events into semi-annual invoices per the cycle configuration.

**Confirms requirement:** Both parts of the example scenario (immediate septic + semi-annual waste) are configurable through the UI using Billing Restrictions and Billing Cycles respectively.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

### AC10 — System generates immediate invoice for septic tank; semi-annual for waste

1. Once restriction and cycle rules are configured (see AC8 above), billing events are processed automatically by the system.
2. When a septic tank emptying event occurs, the system applies the IMMEDIATE restriction and includes it in the next invoice run without waiting for a scheduled cycle.
3. For mixed waste events, the system batches them according to the semi-annual billing cycle and generates the invoice on the scheduled date.

There is no manual "trigger immediate invoice" button in the UI — invoice generation happens when an invoice run is started via the **Invoices** tab > **Invoice Runs** page. The billing restriction and cycle configuration is what controls the behaviour.

**Confirms requirement:** The rules are visible and configurable in the UI. The actual invoice timing behaviour is driven by those rules and executed by the backend during invoice run processing.

**UI Status:** ⚠️ PARTIAL

The configuration is visible; however, users cannot directly observe the runtime enforcement (i.e., see that a specific event was invoiced immediately due to a restriction) from a single screen. The result is visible only in the invoice output after a run.
