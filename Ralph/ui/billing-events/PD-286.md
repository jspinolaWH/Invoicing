## Where to Find This Feature

This feature spans two areas of the app. The configuration lives under the **Master Data** tab (sidebar: **Min. Fees**), where administrators define minimum net amounts per customer type and billing period. The resulting adjustment line items are visible on individual invoices under the **Invoices** tab after an invoice run completes.

## User Flows by Acceptance Criterion

---

### AC1 — Contract-date compliance for mid-season customers

1. Click **Master Data** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Min. Fees**.
3. You will see a table of minimum fee configurations with columns for Customer Type, Threshold, Period, Start Adj., End Adj., Product Code, Active, and Actions.
4. To create a new rule, click **+ Add Config** in the top-right corner. A modal dialog opens.
5. In the modal you will see two checkboxes:
   - **Exempt if contract started after period start** — when ticked, the system will not apply the minimum fee to a customer whose contract began mid-season.
   - **Exempt if contract ended before period end** — when ticked, the system will not apply the minimum fee to a customer whose contract ended mid-season.
6. Check or uncheck these boxes as required, fill in the other fields, and click **Save**.
7. The new row appears in the table and the "Start Adj." and "End Adj." columns show Yes or No accordingly.

**Confirms requirement:** The two contract-adjustment checkboxes let an administrator tell the system to skip the minimum fee for customers who started or ended their contract mid-season, preventing overbilling.

**UI Status:** ⚠️ PARTIAL

The configuration toggles are present, but there is no per-customer override screen and no UI report showing which specific customers were exempted because of these rules during a run. The logic itself is backend-enforced once the config is saved.

### AC2 — Automatic minimum fee calculation adds a top-up surcharge line

1. There is no dedicated screen where a user triggers or views the automatic surcharge calculation independently.
2. The calculation happens in the backend when an invoice run is executed (see AC6 below for how to see the result on a completed invoice).

**Confirms requirement:** N/A — the calculation is invisible to the user until the resulting line item appears on a finished invoice.

**UI Status:** ❌ NOT VISIBLE

The automatic comparison of the invoice total against the minimum threshold and the injection of a top-up line item happens entirely in the backend invoicing engine. There is no progress indicator, log, or user-facing step that shows this calculation taking place. The evidence of the calculation is the resulting line item on the invoice (covered under AC6).

### AC3 — Tariff-based minimum amounts configured in the system

1. Click **Master Data** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Min. Fees**.
3. You will see a table showing all minimum fee rules. Each row shows:
   - **Customer Type** (e.g. RESIDENTIAL, BUSINESS, MUNICIPAL)
   - **Threshold (€ net)** — the minimum net amount for the billing period
   - **Period** (ANNUAL or QUARTERLY)
   - **Product Code** — the product code used when the adjustment line is created
4. To add a new tariff-level minimum, click **+ Add Config**.
5. In the modal, enter the minimum net amount (VAT 0%) in the **Minimum Net Amount** field, select the customer type and period, supply the adjustment product code, and click **Save**.
6. The new row appears immediately in the table.

**Confirms requirement:** Administrators can read and write the minimum charge thresholds that correspond to tariff levels; the system uses these values automatically when calculating invoices.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC4 — Customer contract validation before applying minimum charge

1. Click **Master Data** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Min. Fees**.
3. Click **Edit** on any existing minimum fee config row.
4. In the modal you will see the two contract-date exemption checkboxes:
   - **Exempt if contract started after period start**
   - **Exempt if contract ended before period end**
5. These flags instruct the backend to validate the customer's contract dates before applying the minimum charge.
6. Adjust the checkboxes as needed and click **Save**.

**Confirms requirement:** The checkboxes expose the contract-validation logic so administrators can control whether mid-contract customers are shielded from the minimum fee; the backend then performs the actual contract-date check during the invoice run.

**UI Status:** ⚠️ PARTIAL

The system-level toggle exists, but there is no customer-level screen where a user can see whether a specific customer passed or failed the contract validation, and there is no run-level report listing which customers were exempted due to contract dates.

---

### AC5 — Enable or disable minimum charge per configuration entry

1. Click **Master Data** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Min. Fees**.
3. The **Active** column shows **Yes** for active configs and a dimmed **No** for inactive ones. Inactive rows are shown at 50% opacity.
4. To disable a config, click **Deactivate** on any active row. A confirmation prompt appears asking "Deactivate this minimum fee config?" — click **OK**.
5. The row becomes dimmed and the **Deactivate** button disappears.
6. To re-enable a config (or change its settings), click **Edit** on the row, check the **Active** checkbox in the modal, and click **Save**.

**Confirms requirement:** Administrators can enable or disable individual minimum fee rules through the Active flag, giving companies the flexibility to turn off the minimum fee for certain customer types or periods.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC6 — Minimum charge adjustments appear in invoicing material

1. Click **Invoices** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Invoice List**.
3. You will see a table of all invoices. Click on any invoice row to open the invoice detail page.
4. On the invoice detail page, scroll down to the **Line Items** section. This table shows every line on the invoice including Description, Qty, Unit Price, VAT %, Net amount, Gross amount, Classification, Account, and Cost Center.
5. If the invoice total was below the minimum threshold, the system will have added a line item using the adjustment product code (e.g. MIN_FEE_ADJ). This line appears as a normal line item with the top-up net amount.
6. The total amounts in the **Amount Summary** section below the line items table will include this adjustment.

**Confirms requirement:** Minimum fee top-up lines appear as standard invoice line items in the invoicing material, making them visible in the invoice detail and ensuring they are included in financial totals and exports.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

## Backend-Only Aspects

There is no user-facing step that exposes this process; users see only the resulting adjustment line item on the completed invoice.
