## Where to Find This Feature

This feature lives primarily under the **Operations** tab on the Billing Events list and individual event detail pages. Invoice-level credit and transfer operations are also accessible from the **Invoices** tab, and the **Tools** tab contains a dedicated Responsibility Change tool for bulk ownership transfers.

## User Flows by Acceptance Criterion

---

### AC1 — Copy or transfer invoiced events to the same or a different customer/property

**Via the event detail page (single event transfer):**

1. Click **Operations** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Billing Events**.
3. You will see a table of all billing events with columns for Date, Customer, Product, Status, and Origin.
4. Find an event with status **In Progress** and click the **View** button on that row. (The Transfer button is only available on In Progress events.)
5. You will land on the event detail page. At the top right, you will see a **Transfer** button.
6. Click **Transfer**. A modal dialog opens titled "Transfer Billing Event".
7. The modal shows the current customer number and asks you to search for a **Target Customer** (required) and optionally a **Target Property**.
8. Type a few letters of the target customer's name or number in the search box and select the customer from the dropdown.
9. Optionally search for and select a property belonging to that customer.
10. Enter a **Reason** in the text area (required).
11. Click **Next: Review →**. A review summary appears showing the current customer → new customer, the target property, and the reason.
12. Click **Initiate Transfer**. The event status changes to **Pending Transfer**.
13. Back on the detail page, two new buttons appear: **Confirm Transfer** and **Cancel Transfer**.
14. Click **Confirm Transfer** to finalise the transfer. The event is now assigned to the new customer (and property if selected).

**Via the event list (bulk transfer of multiple events):**

1. Click **Operations** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Billing Events**.
3. Use the checkboxes on the left of each row to select multiple In Progress events.
4. A toolbar appears above the table. Click **Transfer Selected**.
5. A "Transfer N Events" modal opens with the same Target Customer, Target Property, and Reason fields.
6. Fill in the fields and click **Next: Review →**, then **Initiate Transfer for N Events**.

**Confirms requirement:** Users can transfer individual or multiple billing events to a different customer and/or property directly from the billing events pages, satisfying the requirement to transfer via event lines.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

### AC2 — In a crediting situation, events can be transferred to be re-invoiced

1. Click **Operations** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Billing Events**.
3. Find a billing event that has been invoiced (status **Sent** or **Completed**) and click **View** on that row.
4. On the event detail page, you will see a **Credit & Transfer** button at the top right. This button appears only for Sent or Completed events that are not already part of a credit-transfer chain.
5. Click **Credit & Transfer**. A modal opens titled "Credit & Transfer Billing Event".
6. The modal shows a warning explaining that a credit will be issued for the original invoice and a new event will be created for the target customer.
7. A summary of the original event (ID, customer, product, total fees) is displayed.
8. Search for and select the **Target Customer** (required), optionally select a **Target Property**, and enter a **Reason** (required).
9. Click **Confirm Credit & Transfer**.
10. The system issues a credit reversal event for the original customer and creates a new billing event for the target customer, ready to be re-invoiced.

**Confirms requirement:** A user can credit an already-invoiced event and simultaneously transfer it to a different (or the same) customer for re-invoicing in a single action.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

### AC3 — Customer change: original invoice and credit note remain visible for previous customer; new invoice created for new customer

**Via the Credit & Transfer flow (single event):**

After performing a Credit & Transfer (AC2 above):

1. The original event detail page shows a blue information banner: "A credit & transfer was issued for this event."
2. Two buttons appear in the banner: **View Credit Event #[id]** and **View Transfer Event #[id]**, allowing navigation to the credit reversal event and the new event respectively.
3. The credit reversal event shows a yellow banner: "This event is a credit reversal" with a link back to the **View Original Event**.
4. The new (transferred) event shows a green banner: "This event was created via Credit & Transfer" with links to both the original and credit events.
5. The original invoice remains visible under the **Invoices** tab for the previous customer; the credit note is listed on the invoice detail page under a "This invoice has been (partially) credited" yellow banner, with a clickable link to the credit note.

**Via the Responsibility Change tool (bulk ownership transfer):**

1. Click **Tools** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Resp. Change**.
3. Fill in the **From Customer Number**, **To Customer Number**, date range, and reason.
4. Click **Preview Changes**. The system shows a preview table listing all affected events, their statuses, and a note that already-billed events will be excluded from the original customer and correction copies created for the new customer.
5. Click **Apply Change** and confirm. The system shows a result summary: events moved in-place, correction copies created, and originals excluded. The original billing history for the previous customer remains intact.

**Confirms requirement:** Both the single-event Credit & Transfer path and the bulk Responsibility Change tool preserve the original invoice for the previous customer while creating a new event (and eventually invoice) for the new customer.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

### AC4 — Generated events can be transferred or copied to same or different customer or property

1. Click **Operations** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Billing Events**.
3. Find any event with status **In Progress** (these are generated/active events). Click **View**.
4. On the event detail page, click the **Transfer** button.
5. In the Transfer modal, you may choose the **same customer** as the original (to reassign to a different property) or a **different customer**.
6. Select a Target Customer (can be the same customer number), optionally a Target Property, enter a Reason, and click **Next: Review →**, then **Initiate Transfer**.
7. The event moves to Pending Transfer status. Click **Confirm Transfer** to apply.

**Confirms requirement:** The Transfer button is available for In Progress (generated) events and accepts both the same customer (with a different property) and a completely different customer.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

### AC5 — For invoiced and credited events, information remains visible showing what was invoiced and credited

**On the billing event detail page:**

1. Click **Operations** in the top navigation bar, then **Billing Events**, then **View** on any event that has undergone a Credit & Transfer.
2. The event detail page displays colour-coded chain banners:
   - **Blue banner** on the original event: "A credit & transfer was issued for this event" — with buttons to navigate to the credit event and the transfer event.
   - **Yellow banner** on the credit event: "This event is a credit reversal" — with a button to navigate to the original event.
   - **Green banner** on the transferred event: "This event was created via Credit & Transfer" — with buttons to navigate to both original and credit events.
3. Additionally, if a transfer was applied, the event detail shows: "Transferred from: [prior customer number]" and "Prior property: [prior property ID]" as informational banners.

**On the invoice detail page:**

1. Click **Invoices** in the top navigation bar, then **Invoice List** in the sidebar.
2. Click on any invoice that has a credit note.
3. The invoice detail page shows a yellow banner at the top: "This invoice has been (partially) credited" listing all credit notes with clickable links, issue dates, and net amounts.

**On the customer billing profile page:**

1. Click **Customers & Config** in the top navigation bar, then **Billing Profiles** in the sidebar.
2. Click on a customer row to open their billing profile.
3. Click the **Credit History** tab. A table lists all credit notes for that customer, including Credit Note Number, Date, Original Invoice Number, Net Amount, Gross Amount, and Status.

**Confirms requirement:** All three surfaces (billing event detail banners, invoice detail credit note list, and customer credit history tab) together give users full visibility into what was invoiced, credited, and re-invoiced for each customer.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

### AC6 — Invoicing system integration

1. Click **Invoices** in the top navigation bar, then **Invoice List** in the sidebar.
2. Find any invoice with status **Sent** or **Completed** and click on it.
3. On the invoice detail page, scroll down to find the **Invoice Transmit** panel (rendered by the InvoiceTransmitPanel component). This panel manages the connection to the external invoicing/e-invoicing system.
4. The panel shows the current transmission status, an external reference number if available, and options to transmit or recall the invoice from the external system (if permitted).

**Confirms requirement:** The transmit panel on the invoice detail page surfaces the integration link between the invoicing system and external e-invoicing operators.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE
