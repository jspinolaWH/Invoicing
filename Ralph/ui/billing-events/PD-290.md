## Where to Find This Feature

This feature lives under the **Customers & Config** tab. Bundling rules are configured per customer from the customer's billing profile page, accessed by clicking a customer row in the Billing Profiles list. The effect of bundling is visible on generated invoices in the **Invoices** tab.

## User Flows by Acceptance Criterion

---

### AC1 — Flexible invoicing structure that allows grouping of events per customer

1. Click **Customers & Config** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Billing Profiles**.
3. You will see a table of customers. Find the customer you want to configure and click **View Billing Profile**.
4. On the billing profile page, just below the customer name heading, click the **Bundling Rules** button.
5. You will arrive at the Bundling Rules page for that customer, which shows a table with columns for Product Group, Bundling Type, Description, and Actions.
6. The page header states "Configure how billing events are grouped on invoices for this customer," confirming the flexible per-customer grouping structure is in place.

**Confirms requirement:** The Bundling Rules page exists per customer and allows the company to define how billing events are grouped on that customer's invoices.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC2 — Users can define grouping rules based on product types, services, or other predefined criteria

1. Click **Customers & Config** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Billing Profiles**.
3. Click **View Billing Profile** for the target customer.
4. Click the **Bundling Rules** button near the top of the billing profile page.
5. At the bottom of the Bundling Rules page, you will see an "Add Product Group" input field and a "Type" dropdown.
6. In the **Add Product Group** field, type the product group code (for example, CONTAINER_EMPTYING).
7. In the **Type** dropdown, choose either **SINGLE_LINE** (bundle all matching events into one line) or **SEPARATE** (keep each event as its own line).
8. Click **Add Row**. The new rule appears in the table above.
9. Repeat for as many product groups as needed (e.g. BASE_SERVICE_FEE as SEPARATE, PALLET_DELIVERY as SEPARATE).
10. Click **Save Changes** to persist all rules.

**Confirms requirement:** Users can define grouping rules by product category; each rule specifies whether events of that product group are consolidated or listed individually.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC3 — Customer-specific bundling; changes to grouping do not affect underlying event data

1. Click **Customers & Config** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Billing Profiles**.
3. Click **View Billing Profile** for the target customer.
4. Click **Bundling Rules**.
5. Change an existing rule's bundling type by clicking its **SINGLE_LINE** or **SEPARATE** toggle button — it flips between the two values.
6. Click **Save Changes**. A green success message "Bundling rules saved." appears.
7. Navigate to **Operations** > **Billing Events** and open any billing event for this customer. You will see all event data (dates, prices, quantities) is unchanged — only the invoice presentation is affected.

**Confirms requirement:** Bundling configuration is stored separately from event data; toggling it does not alter any billing event records.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC4 — Customer-specific bundling rules: specify SINGLE_LINE vs SEPARATE per event type

1. Click **Customers & Config** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Billing Profiles**.
3. Click **View Billing Profile** for the target customer.
4. Click **Bundling Rules**.
5. The table shows each product group with its current Bundling Type shown as a clickable button.
6. Click any type button to toggle it between **SINGLE_LINE** and **SEPARATE**.
7. You can also remove a rule entirely by clicking the **Remove** button on any row.
8. Click **Save Changes** to apply.

**Confirms requirement:** Each product group for a customer has an explicit SINGLE_LINE or SEPARATE setting that can be individually configured and saved.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC5 — Flexible grouping options: emptying events as one line, base fees separate, pallets individual

1. Click **Customers & Config** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Billing Profiles**.
3. Click **View Billing Profile** for the target customer.
4. Click **Bundling Rules**.
5. In the "Add Product Group" field, type **CONTAINER_EMPTYING**; set Type to **SINGLE_LINE**; click **Add Row**.
6. In the "Add Product Group" field, type **BASE_SERVICE_FEE**; set Type to **SEPARATE**; click **Add Row**.
7. In the "Add Product Group" field, type **PALLET_DELIVERY**; set Type to **SEPARATE**; click **Add Row**.
8. Click **Save Changes**. You will see all three rules in the table — exactly matching the example from the requirement (emptying bundled into one line, base fees and pallet deliveries listed separately).

**Confirms requirement:** The UI supports the exact scenario described — emptying events bundled, base fees separate, pallet deliveries individual — by entering each product group and its bundling type.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC6 — Bundling rules are automatically applied when invoices are generated

1. After setting bundling rules for a customer (see AC4/AC5), click **Invoices** in the top navigation bar.
2. In the left sidebar, click **Generate Invoice**.
3. Enter the Customer ID and a billing period date range, then click **Preview Invoice**.
4. A preview panel appears showing invoice line items. Any line items that were bundled by the rules will display a small **B** badge next to their description in the Line Items table.
5. Click **Confirm & Generate** to create the invoice.
6. Navigate to **Invoice List** in the sidebar, find the new invoice, and click it to open the detail.
7. The Line Items table shows consolidated lines for product groups with SINGLE_LINE rules (with a **B** badge), and individual lines for SEPARATE groups.

**Confirms requirement:** The bundling rules are applied automatically during invoice generation — no manual grouping step is needed, and the result is visible on the generated invoice.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC7 — Customization per waste management company / business requirements

1. Navigate to **Customers & Config** > **Billing Profiles**.
2. Each customer row has its own **View Billing Profile** link, leading to that customer's own **Bundling Rules** page.
3. Different customers can have entirely different bundling rule sets — one customer may have all groups as SINGLE_LINE, another may have all groups as SEPARATE, with no cross-customer impact.

**Confirms requirement:** Each customer maintains its own independent bundling configuration, allowing each waste management company to tailor invoice layouts to its own contracts and preferences.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC8 — Bundled events remain separate in financial records while appearing grouped on invoices

1. Navigate to **Invoices** > **Invoice List** and open any invoice for a customer that has SINGLE_LINE bundling rules.
2. In the Line Items table, bundled lines display a small **B** badge, indicating they represent multiple underlying events combined into one invoice line.
3. Navigate to **Operations** > **Billing Events** and filter or browse events for the same customer. Each event still appears as its own individual record with its own date, product, price, and status — none are merged or deleted.

**Confirms requirement:** The Invoice Detail shows consolidated lines, while the Billing Events list shows every event record individually — the underlying data is preserved, only the invoice presentation is grouped.

**UI Status:** ✅ VISIBLE

---

### AC9 — Audit and compliance: system logs all bundling configurations and changes

1. Navigate to **Customers & Config** > **Billing Profiles** and open any customer's billing profile.
2. Click the **Bundling Rules** button to open the bundling rules page.
3. The page shows the current configuration but provides no history tab, no change log, and no audit trail of who changed which rule and when.

**Confirms requirement:** There is no visible audit log for bundling rule changes in the UI. The feature is not surfaced on the Bundling Rules page.

**UI Status:** ⚠️ PARTIAL

**What is missing:** The Bundling Rules page has no audit or change history section. There is no way for a user to see who created, modified, or deleted a bundling rule, or when those actions occurred. The backend may log these changes, but no UI surface exposes that log.
